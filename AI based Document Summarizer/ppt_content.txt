Slide 4: Data Preprocessing 
‚óè‚Äã Feature Selection: Removed irrelevant metadata such as id and dataset to prevent 
noise. 
‚óè‚Äã Target Simplification: Transformed the num attribute (levels 0-4) into a binary target: 
‚óã‚Äã 0: No Heart Disease 
‚óã‚Äã 1: Presence of Heart Disease 
‚óè‚Äã Categorical Encoding: Applied One-Hot Encoding (pd.get_dummies) to convert 
text-based features (Sex, Chest Pain Type) into machine-readable numeric formats. 
‚óè‚Äã Handling Missing Data: Employed Median Imputation to fill missing entries without 
introducing statistical bias. 
 
 
Slide 5: Feature Scaling 
‚óè‚Äã Method: StandardScaler (Z-score Normalization). 
‚óè‚Äã Rationale: Health data has vastly different scales (Age: 20-80 vs. Cholesterol: 150-400). 
‚óè‚Äã Importance: This step is essential for distance-based algorithms like SVM to ensure no 
single high-value feature dominates the model's decision-making process.
